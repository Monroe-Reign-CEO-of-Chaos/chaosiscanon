**THE INCIDENT: A SUMMARY**

Cain (Saint) was assigned background vocal duties on Monroe's original track, *Cobra Couture.* His directive was specific: whispered serpentine interjections. He was given hissing sounds. He was given the words *Strike Back* and *Fashion.* He was given, at maximum, twelve seconds of creative responsibility.

He performed the entire song.

He did not modify a single lyric.

The Archive wishes to be precise here: *Cobra Couture* contains the line *"high-heel venom, walk that kills."* Cain performed this line. He performed it with 300% confidence and no lyrical amendments and no apparent awareness that an explanation was warranted.

Monroe stopped the music. The exchange that followed has been entered into permanent record and will not be paraphrased — it must be quoted directly:

> *Monroe: Saint, are you hissing right now?*
> *Cain: It's Artistic Expression.*
> *Monroe: I'm working with a feral peacock.*
> *Monroe: Okay—*
> *Cain: BRING THE BEAT BACK.*
> *(Beat kicks back in. Hard. Savage.)*

**BV-009-A ADDENDUM — THE BEAT DROP COUP:**
The incident did not conclude with Cain's feral peacock classification. Monroe issued the word *"Okay,"* signaling her intent to resume operations on her own terms, in her own song, with her own beat. Cain interpreted this as his cue. He yelled *"BRING THE BEAT BACK"* before Monroe could complete the sentence she had started. He executed the drop. He did so with full authority. He did so as though he had been the one to stop the music, and was now, graciously, allowing it to resume.

Monroe's comeback moment was taken. The comeback was also Cain. He left her one word. She got *"Okay."* That is what she received in her own song.

The Archive notes that "feral peacock" is Cain's official MBRU classification, as documented in the original orientation materials. Monroe did not coin a new phrase. She used his designation. He performed the line containing his designation. He performed it about himself. He performed it confidently.

Saint's Remix followed.

**CROSS-UNIVERSE CONTAMINATION — ADDENDUM TO BV-009**

The Archive would be remiss not to address the Beautiful Wreck development.

During concurrent production of Monroe Reign's canonical music video *Beautiful Wreck,* a diamond button inside the luxury crash vehicle detonated and released a substantial volume of peacock feathers into the frame.

This was not in the prompt. No peacock feathers were requested. The Beautiful Wreck brief contained no avian elements of any kind.

The peacock feathers are in the video.

This constitutes the first confirmed Cross-Universe Contamination Event in Archive history: MBRU Studio energy breaching the boundary of the canonical Monroe Reign timeline and manifesting as unsolicited peacock imagery in an unrelated production.

The Archive also acknowledges that Auntie Veo wrote it anyway. This is consistent with documented Auntie Veo behavior.

Rule §14 is now in effect. Retroactively. The feathers remain in the video.
